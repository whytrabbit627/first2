## The Case for Babywearing

Carrying your baby close is one of the most consistently effective tools for calming a fussy infant. Research supports what parents have known for generations: babies who are carried cry less, sleep more, and develop confidence more readily. For partners and caregivers who want to be genuinely hands-on in the early months, a carrier makes that possible while still getting things done.

The Ergobaby Omni 360 is designed to make babywearing genuinely comfortable for the wearer — something that matters a great deal when you're doing it for hours at a time.

## What "Omni 360" Means

The 360 refers to the four carry positions the carrier supports:

1. **Front-facing in (newborn):** Baby faces your chest, legs in the frog position. Safe from birth and ideal for younger babies who need full neck and head support.
2. **Front-facing out:** Baby faces the world. Best once baby has good head control (typically 4–6 months) and is alert enough to benefit from the outward-facing view.
3. **Hip carry:** Baby sits on your hip, supported by the carrier structure. Useful for older babies who want variety.
4. **Back carry:** Baby rides on your back, freeing both hands and distributing weight more comfortably for longer carries with heavier toddlers.

The critical advantage of the Omni 360 over some competing carriers is that all four positions are available without purchasing a separate newborn insert. The internal adjustment adapts the carrier to newborn proportions without an add-on piece.

## Ergonomics — Wearer and Baby

Ergobaby built their reputation on ergonomic design. The Omni 360 distributes baby's weight across the wearer's hips and shoulders via a padded waistband and wide, cushioned shoulder straps. For extended carries, this matters enormously — cheaper carriers that concentrate weight on the shoulders become genuinely uncomfortable within an hour.

For baby, the seat is designed to position baby in the "M position" (also called the ergonomic or frog position) — hips spread, knees higher than the bottom. This supports healthy hip development and is recommended by paediatric hip dysplasia organisations.

## The Washable, Adjustable Design

The Omni 360 is machine washable on a gentle cycle, which is important given the level of spit-up, drool, and general mess that accumulates in a carrier. The waistband and shoulder straps adjust to fit a wide range of body sizes, and the carrier can be shared between parents of different builds without significant re-adjustment time.

## One Honest Limitation

The Omni 360 is not a minimalist carrier. It has structure, padding, and several adjustment points that make setup take slightly longer than a simple wrap or ring sling. For carriers who want something they can throw on in seconds, a simpler option may suit better day-to-day. The Omni 360 rewards those who take a few sessions to learn the fit — after that, it becomes quick to put on.

## Bottom Line

For families who want one carrier that genuinely works from newborn through toddlerhood, the Ergobaby Omni 360 is one of the most complete options available. It's well-made, comfortable for extended wear, and doesn't require additional purchases for different stages. Both parents can use it comfortably with simple adjustments.
