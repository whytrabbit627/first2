## When Do You Need a High Chair?

Most families introduce a high chair around **5–6 months** — when baby can sit supported and shows readiness for solid foods. You'll use it multiple times a day until around 2–3 years old, so it's worth choosing carefully.

## What to Look For

**Safety:** Five-point harness, wide stable base, no sharp edges. Look for JPMA certification.

**Ease of cleaning:** This is non-negotiable. Seat covers with lots of crevices accumulate food and become unsanitary fast. One-piece trays that go in the dishwasher and seamless seat surfaces make your life significantly easier.

**Adjustability:** Seat height and footrest adjustment matter for proper ergonomics — and become more important as your child grows. A chair that adjusts to multiple positions gives you years of use.

**Footrest:** Often overlooked. A supported foot position helps babies stabilize their core, which matters for both swallowing safely and self-feeding. Look for an adjustable footrest.

**Size:** Standard high chairs are large. If space is tight, look at convertible options or slim profiles.

## Four Well-Regarded Options

### OXO Tot Sprout — Best All-Around
Widely recommended by occupational therapists for its adjustable seat, footrest, and back recline. One-piece wipeable seat, removable dishwasher-safe tray insert. Grows with baby through toddlerhood. Mid-range price.

### Stokke Tripp Trapp — Best Long-Term Value
Converts from high chair to toddler seat to adult chair. Fully adjustable seat and footrest positions. Minimalist Scandinavian design. Expensive upfront but built to last decades. Note: requires separate baby set for infants under 6 months.

### IKEA ANTILOP — Best Budget Option
The cult classic. Simple, lightweight, easy to clean, dishwasher-safe tray. Lacks adjustability, but at the price point it's hard to beat for functionality. Many families have one as a second chair or travel option.

### Graco Slim Spaces — Best for Small Kitchens
Folds flat against the wall when not in use. Good adjustability, five-point harness, dishwasher-safe tray. Significantly slimmer footprint than most standard high chairs.

## Setup and Safety Tips

- Always use the harness, even if your baby seems stable — babies can lean and fall unexpectedly
- Position the tray so baby's elbows rest comfortably at tray height
- Keep the high chair away from walls and counters baby can push off of
- Clean the tray and seat after every meal — food left in crevices grows mold quickly

## When to Stop Using a High Chair

Most children transition to a booster seat at the table around 2–3 years old, when they can climb in and out independently and sit at a regular table comfortably. Many convertible high chairs include a toddler booster mode that extends their useful life.
