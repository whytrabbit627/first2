## What Is the Hatch Rest+?

The Hatch Rest+ is a two-in-one sound machine and night light designed to grow with your child from the newborn phase through toddlerhood and beyond. Unlike a simple white noise machine that just plays one sound on a loop, the Rest+ connects to your phone via the Hatch Sleep app, giving you full control over sound type, volume, light colour, and brightness — all without stepping foot in the nursery.

The "plus" in the name refers to the built-in time-to-rise feature: as your toddler gets older, you can program the light to turn green at an acceptable wake time, gradually teaching them to stay in bed until the light changes. Most families get years of use out of a single device.

## Why Sound Matters for Baby Sleep

Newborns spend nine months surrounded by sound in the womb — your heartbeat, the rush of blood flow, muffled external noise. Silence is actually foreign and often unsettling for them. White noise recreates something closer to that familiar environment, which is why so many babies sleep better and longer with it playing.

Beyond helping baby fall asleep, continuous white noise serves a practical household function: it masks the inevitable sounds of life — a door closing, a sibling's voice, a delivery at the door — that might otherwise wake a lightly sleeping baby.

## What Sets the Hatch Apart

Several features make the Rest+ particularly thoughtful for new parents:

**Remote control via app.** When baby stirs at 4am, you can turn the sound up slightly from your phone without entering the room and risking a full wake-up. For parents doing sleep training, this is genuinely useful.

**Soft night light for feeds.** The dimmable, warm-toned light is bright enough to see what you're doing during a nappy change or feed without being harsh enough to signal "daytime" to a baby's developing circadian system. It's a small detail that makes a real difference at 2am.

**Schedules.** Set the sound to turn on automatically at bedtime and off in the morning. The app supports multiple routines, so you can have different settings for nap time and overnight.

**Sound variety.** White noise, brown noise, ocean sounds, rain, and lullabies are all available. Most sleep consultants recommend a steady, non-melodic sound (white or brown noise) for overnight sleep.

## Honest Downsides

The Hatch requires Wi-Fi and the app to use its smarter features — if your internet goes down, you can still use it manually, but it's less convenient. The subscription (Hatch+) unlocks additional sounds and features; the base experience without it is solid but limited.

It's also worth noting that any sound machine will work for masking noise — you don't *need* the Hatch specifically. If budget is a concern, a cheaper dedicated white noise machine will do the core job. The Hatch earns its price through the night light integration, the app control, and the long useful lifespan.

## Practical Tips

- Place it at least 2 metres from baby's head and set volume at a conversational level (around 50–60dB)
- Use white or brown noise for sleep, not lullabies — melodic sounds can become sleep associations that require your involvement to restart
- Set up the schedule before baby arrives so it's one less thing to configure in the fog of the newborn phase
- The Rest+ charges via USB-C and has a backup battery for brief outages

## Bottom Line

The Hatch Rest+ is the most thoughtful sound machine and night light combination on the market. For families who want one device that handles white noise, nighttime visibility, and eventually wake-time training, it's a genuinely excellent long-term investment.
