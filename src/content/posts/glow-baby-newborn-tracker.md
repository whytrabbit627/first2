## Why Track in the Newborn Phase

In the first weeks home from hospital, tracking feeds and output is not optional — it's medically necessary. Your paediatrician will ask how many feeds baby is getting in 24 hours, how long each side for breastfeeding, how many wet and dirty nappies. Accurate answers to these questions are how you and your provider know that breastfeeding is working and that baby is getting enough.

Beyond the medical necessity, tracking serves a practical function when you're sleep-deprived: you cannot reliably remember which side you fed on last at 4am when you're running on two hours of sleep. An app does this reliably; your brain does not.

## What Glow Baby Tracks

**Feeds.** Breast (left/right, duration), bottle (volume), or both. Breastfeeding logs remind you which side to start the next feed. Bottle tracking gives daily volume totals.

**Nappy changes.** Wet, dirty, or both. The app tracks daily totals, which is useful for the early days when nappy count is a key indicator of adequate intake.

**Sleep.** Start and end times for each sleep session. The day view shows total sleep in 24 hours and individual sleep durations.

**Pumping sessions.** Volume per session and per side. For exclusively pumping parents or those building a stash, this history is useful for identifying trends and troubleshooting supply.

**Growth.** Weight and length measurements can be logged, with growth chart visualisation. A useful way to see your baby's trajectory between paediatric appointments.

**Milestones.** Log and photograph first smile, first roll, first word — the standard developmental milestones in a format that also captures memories.

## Partner Access

Glow Baby supports multiple users on the same account, so both parents can log from their own phones and see each other's entries in real time. This is genuinely useful for overnight handoffs: the incoming parent can see at a glance when the last feed happened, how long it lasted, and which side, without waking the outgoing parent to ask.

The shared log also reduces the common new-parent communication friction: "I thought you logged the feed" / "I thought you did." One app, shared between you, removes the ambiguity.

## Interface and Ease of Use

Glow Baby's interface is clean and fast. The one-tap logging for the most common activities (start/stop feed, wet nappy, start/stop sleep) means logging takes 3–5 seconds rather than a menu navigation that feels prohibitive at 3am.

The timeline view is clear — the day laid out chronologically, with colour-coded blocks for different activity types. The weekly summaries are useful for spotting patterns.

## Free vs. Premium

The free version covers all core tracking. Glow Baby Premium adds enhanced insights, more detailed reports, and some additional features. For most families, the free version is sufficient.

## When to Stop Tracking

Tracking is most valuable in the newborn phase — roughly the first 8–12 weeks for most families. As breastfeeding is established, as patterns emerge and become predictable, the necessity (and the cognitive bandwidth available for logging) both decrease. Many parents find they naturally stop around 3–4 months when things have stabilised.

There's no obligation to track indefinitely. Use it as a tool for the phase when you need the data, and let it go when it's no longer adding value.

## Bottom Line

Glow Baby is a well-designed, partner-friendly newborn tracker that covers everything you need to monitor in the first weeks. Download it before baby arrives, set up both parent accounts, and start logging from day one. The data will be invaluable for early paediatric appointments and for your own peace of mind.
