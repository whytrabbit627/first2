## What Is the SNOO?

The SNOO Smart Sleeper is a motorised bassinet designed by Dr. Harvey Karp — the paediatrician behind *The Happiest Baby on the Block* — in collaboration with MIT engineers. It's not just a bassinet that rocks. It's a bassinet that listens.

When your baby makes a sound that resembles crying, the SNOO's built-in microphone detects it and automatically responds by increasing both the rocking speed and white noise level. If baby settles, it eases back down. If crying continues, it escalates through a sequence of five levels before alerting you that your baby needs attention. The idea is to bridge the gap between a minor stir and a full wake-up — and for many families, it works remarkably well.

## Who It's For

The SNOO is built for newborns up to around 6 months, or until baby starts pushing up on their hands and knees. It's particularly well-suited to:

- **Exhausted parents** trying to extend sleep stretches in those brutal early weeks
- **Parents of colicky or high-needs babies** who are otherwise difficult to settle
- **Partners on solo night shifts** who need a reliable tool when settling by hand isn't working

It's less essential if you have a naturally calm baby who settles easily, or if you're philosophically committed to contact sleeping.

## The Case For It

The SNOO has a genuine body of evidence behind it. A clinical study published in the *Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine* found that SNOO-using families got significantly more sleep in the early weeks than those using standard bassinets. Parents consistently report that the SNOO adds 1–2 hours of sleep per night during the newborn period — and at 3am, that feels enormous.

The built-in swaddle system is also a meaningful safety feature. The SNOO sack clips into the bassinet in a way that keeps baby on their back, eliminating the rolling risk that makes loose swaddles unsafe as babies get stronger.

## The Case Against It

Let's be honest: the SNOO is expensive. The retail price puts it out of reach for many families without careful budgeting or a generous gift registry. A few things worth knowing:

- **It's available to rent.** Happiest Baby offers a rental programme at a fraction of the purchase price — ideal if you're unsure how much use you'll get, or if you already have a second baby in mind.
- **Some babies don't take to it.** The rocking motion works brilliantly for most newborns, but a minority seem genuinely unsettled by it. Most rental and purchase programmes offer return windows — check the terms before you commit.
- **Dependency concerns are mostly unfounded.** Many parents worry their baby will refuse to sleep anywhere else. The SNOO app has a "weaning mode" that gradually reduces responsiveness to help with the transition out of the bassinet.

## Practical Notes

- The SNOO app (iOS and Android) lets you adjust baseline levels and lock the soothing so it doesn't automatically escalate at night
- It folds flat for travel, though it's substantial
- The sack is sold separately and comes in multiple sizes — buy at least two
- White noise from the SNOO is loud enough that you won't need a separate machine while baby is in it

## Bottom Line

If it fits your budget (or you can rent it), the SNOO is one of the few baby products that actually delivers on a big promise: more sleep for everyone. It won't work for every baby, but the rental option makes it low-risk to try. Most parents who use it say the same thing: they wish they'd had it from day one.
