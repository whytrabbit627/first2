## What Makes the mamaRoo Different

Most baby swings move in a single arc — back and forth, side to side. The 4moms mamaRoo 4 moves in five distinct patterns designed to mimic the motions parents naturally use to soothe babies:

- **Car ride:** Smooth, even horizontal glide
- **Kangaroo:** Bouncy up-and-down movement
- **Tree swing:** Gentle side-to-side arc
- **Rock-a-bye:** Slow, rhythmic rocking
- **Wave:** Flowing figure-eight motion

Each pattern runs at five speed levels, and the seat has four recline positions. Built-in white noise options (rain, ocean, birds) can play simultaneously. The combination gives you significantly more variables to work with than a standard swing — which matters when you're trying to crack the code on what your specific baby responds to.

## The App Control Advantage

The mamaRoo 4 connects to the 4moms app via Bluetooth. From your phone, you can change the motion, speed, sound, and volume without touching the unit. When a sleeping baby is inches from the controls, this is not a minor convenience — it's the difference between adjusting a setting and triggering a full wake-up.

The app also lets you save favourite combinations (motion + speed + sound) for one-tap recall. After a few days of experimenting, most parents find one or two configurations their baby settles best to and use those consistently.

## Who It Suits

The mamaRoo is particularly well-suited to babies who haven't responded to traditional swings. Its multi-directional movement seems to engage babies who find simple back-and-forth motion insufficient — particularly fussy or colicky babies, and those who are used to being bounced on a knee or moved in varied ways.

It's also suited to parents who value hands-free time during the newborn phase. A swing that reliably keeps baby content for 20–30 minutes creates meaningful windows for eating, showering, or sleeping.

## Practical Considerations

**Footprint:** The mamaRoo's sculptural design is more compact than traditional pendulum swings, which have wide side-to-side bases. In a smaller home or flat, this matters.

**Weight limit:** 11kg (25lb), which means most babies will outgrow it between 6–9 months.

**Harness:** The five-point harness is secure but takes some practice to fasten one-handed — you'll get faster with repetition.

**Noise level:** Quieter than many swing motors. The built-in white noise, if used, is gentle.

## Not a Guarantee

It's worth being honest: some babies simply don't take to any swing, and some take a few days to warm to the mamaRoo. If your baby has strong preferences about how they're held or soothed, a swing may not provide reliable settling regardless of features.

Given the price point, consider checking whether your local parent group or community has a unit to borrow before purchasing. Secondhand mamaRoos are widely available and hold up well.

## Bottom Line

For the right baby — and many newborns are the right baby — the mamaRoo is a genuinely useful soothing tool that buys parents time and reduces the constant physical demand of the newborn phase. The movement variety and app control make it meaningfully better than a basic swing. Worth serious consideration for your registry or as a secondhand purchase.
