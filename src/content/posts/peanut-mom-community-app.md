## The Problem Peanut Solves

One of the most consistent themes in postpartum wellbeing research is social isolation. New motherhood often narrows your world dramatically: maternity leave separates you from work colleagues, the logistics of a baby limit your mobility, and your existing friendships (with people who don't have babies) may feel suddenly misaligned.

The antidote — genuine connection with other parents at the same stage — is obvious in principle and surprisingly hard to achieve in practice. Most people don't live near the friends they want to see; parent-and-baby groups can feel awkward or cliquey; the effort required to maintain existing friendships while sleep-deprived and breastfeeding is real.

Peanut is designed to solve this by applying the dating app model — swipe-based matching, mutual interest, message when there's a connection — to finding mum friends.

## How It Works

Peanut is built around your geographic location. After creating a profile (name, photo, children's ages, interests), you see a feed of women nearby who are also using the app. The swipe mechanic (like/pass) creates mutual matches before a conversation can start, which removes some of the awkwardness of cold-messaging a stranger.

Beyond the matching mechanic, Peanut has several other features:

**Groups (Pods).** Topic-based communities covering virtually every aspect of motherhood and fertility: breastfeeding, sleep training, single parenting, specific cities and neighbourhoods, pregnancy after loss, and hundreds more. Groups are moderated and can be a useful source of peer support and practical advice.

**Mentor system.** More experienced mothers can volunteer as mentors in specific areas (breastfeeding, feeding, sleep). This is a nice addition — peer-to-peer guidance from someone who has been through it is often more practically useful than generic advice.

**Live sessions.** Online events and discussions hosted within the app. Useful for those who want community without the logistics of in-person meetups.

## Who Gets the Most from It

Peanut tends to work best for:
- People in their second or third trimester who want to build connections before birth
- Those in the newborn phase who are feeling isolated and want to find local peers
- Mothers who have relocated and have limited existing social support
- People who've found traditional mother-and-baby groups inaccessible (too far away, wrong fit, intimidating)

It's less useful if you already have a strong local community of parents at the same stage, or if in-person groups work well for you.

## Realistic Expectations

Peanut is a tool for finding potential connections — not a guarantee of instant friendship. Like any social platform, results depend heavily on who is using it in your area. In major cities, there are usually enough active users to find good matches; in rural or suburban areas, the pool may be smaller.

Many people exchange a few messages without it progressing to a real meetup. That's normal. Treat it like dating: some matches lead somewhere, most don't, and that doesn't reflect on you.

## Privacy Considerations

Peanut asks for your location and shows your general area to other users. It doesn't show your exact address, but it does indicate your neighbourhood. Worth knowing, particularly for people who are cautious about location-sharing apps.

## Bottom Line

Social connection is one of the most powerful determinants of postpartum wellbeing, and Peanut makes finding local mum friends more accessible than it would otherwise be. If you're feeling isolated or anticipate that you might be, downloading it during pregnancy and making a few connections before birth is worth 20 minutes of your time.
