## What PSI Is

Postpartum Support International is a non-profit organisation founded in 1987 with a single purpose: to ensure that no parent suffers alone from a perinatal mental health condition. It's the largest organisation of its kind, with trained volunteers, a provider directory, online support groups, and resources in multiple languages.

Their helpline — **1-800-944-4773** — is staffed by trained volunteers who have personal experience with postpartum mental health challenges. They're not therapists, but they listen without judgment and can help you navigate to professional support in your area.

## Who It's For

PSI's resources are designed for:

- **New and recent mothers** experiencing postpartum depression, anxiety, rage, OCD, or psychosis
- **Partners** experiencing their own postpartum adjustment difficulties (paternal postpartum depression is real and underdiagnosed — PSI has specific resources for dads and non-birthing partners)
- **Pregnant people** experiencing prenatal depression or anxiety (perinatal mental health conditions don't only begin after birth)
- **Anyone who is worried about a partner or loved one** and wants guidance on how to help

## The Helpline

The PSI helpline operates Monday–Friday, 9am–9pm Eastern (English and Spanish). Callers are connected with a trained volunteer who:

- Listens without judgment
- Provides information about postpartum mental health conditions
- Helps identify local professional resources — therapists, psychiatrists, support groups — who specialise in perinatal mental health
- Can offer basic guidance on next steps

The helpline is free. It's not a crisis line, but it's the right first call for someone who isn't sure whether what they're experiencing is "normal" or whether they need professional help.

## Online Support Groups

PSI offers free weekly online support groups via Zoom, organised by condition and demographic. Groups include:
- General postpartum support
- Pregnancy and infant loss
- Support for dads and partners
- Spanish-language groups
- LGBTQ+ specific groups
- Groups for parents in the NICU

Finding a community of people who genuinely understand your experience — not just "I know it's hard" but first-hand knowledge — is often the most meaningful form of support during a mental health crisis.

## The Provider Directory

PSI maintains a searchable database of mental health providers who specifically work with perinatal clients. Finding a therapist who understands the perinatal period is meaningfully different from finding a general therapist — the specificity of the training matters for treatment quality.

## What Postpartum Mental Health Conditions Look Like

Many parents don't reach out because they don't recognise their symptoms as a mental health condition, or because they fear judgment. PSI resources address:

- **Postpartum depression:** Not just sadness. Includes numbness, disconnection from baby, irritability, anxiety, sleep problems (beyond what baby causes), intrusive thoughts
- **Postpartum anxiety:** Excessive worry, inability to rest even when baby sleeps, physical symptoms (racing heart, shortness of breath)
- **Postpartum rage:** Anger that feels disproportionate, frightening, or out of character
- **Postpartum OCD:** Intrusive, unwanted thoughts (often about harming baby) that are deeply distressing and contrary to the parent's values
- **Postpartum psychosis:** Rare but a medical emergency — hallucinations, confusion, rapid mood swings. Seek emergency care immediately.

## Bottom Line

If you're struggling after birth — whether two weeks in or six months in — PSI is a trustworthy first stop. Call the helpline, browse the website, or join an online group. You don't need to meet a diagnostic threshold to reach out. If something feels wrong, that's enough.
