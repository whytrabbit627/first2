## Why Babywearing Workouts Work

Finding time to exercise with a baby is genuinely hard. Babywearing workouts solve two problems at once: your baby is content (close contact is calming for most infants), and you're moving your body. Many babies who won't tolerate a bouncer or play mat are perfectly happy in a carrier while you exercise.

## Safety First: When Is It Appropriate?

**Baby's readiness:**
- Baby should have strong head and neck control — typically 4–6 months for most exercises, though gentle walking with proper head support is fine earlier
- For bouncing or higher-impact moves, wait until baby can hold their head steadily (around 4–6 months)
- Stop if your baby seems uncomfortable, fussy, or if their head is flopping

**Your readiness:**
- Start with low-impact activities — walking — before progressing to anything more demanding
- Wait until you've been cleared for exercise (typically 6–12 weeks postpartum depending on delivery)
- Avoid high-impact babywearing if you have pelvic floor symptoms

**Carrier fit:** Make sure the carrier is properly fitted. Baby should be positioned high enough to kiss, with their chin off their chest (T.I.C.K.S. safety rules).

## Best Exercises for Babywearing

### Walking (Any Age)
The most accessible babywearing workout. Brisk walking is low-impact, counts as moderate cardio, and most carriers are designed for walking. Start with 20–30 minutes and build from there.

### Squats and Lunges (4+ Months)
Bodyweight squats and reverse lunges work well with a carrier — the added weight of your baby increases resistance naturally.
- **Squat variation:** Keep back straight, weight in heels, lower until thighs are parallel to the floor
- **Lunge:** Step back into a reverse lunge, which is more stable than forward lunges with a baby

### Standing Core Work (4+ Months)
Avoid traditional crunches — focus on standing core engagement:
- **Standing pelvic tilts:** Subtle, safe, effective for deep core
- **Side bends:** Slow, controlled, works obliques
- **Farmer's carries:** Walking with the carrier already qualifies — add intentional bracing

### Step-Ups (5+ Months)
Use a step or low platform. Step up one foot at a time, controlled descent. Great for glutes and improves balance.

### Gentle Yoga Poses (4+ Months)
Standing yoga — warrior poses, tree pose for balance — adapt well to babywearing. Avoid inversions or forward folds that compress the baby.

## Exercises to Avoid

- High-impact running or jumping with a baby under 6 months (or not yet sitting independently)
- Forward bends where baby's head could compress
- Anything requiring you to suddenly stop, twist sharply, or fall — your center of gravity shifts with a baby in front

## Making It a Habit

**Morning walk routine:** 30 minutes of brisk walking with baby in the carrier is a complete workout for many parents. Fresh air, movement, and baby content — hard to beat.

**Stair workout:** If you have stairs at home, carrier-based step-ups are efficient. 3 sets of 10 per leg, 3 times per week.

**Join a local babywearing fitness class:** Many communities have stroller fitness or baby-and-me yoga/fitness classes. Check local community centers, rec centers, or search for "postnatal fitness" near you.

The goal isn't performance — it's consistency. A 20-minute walk every day does more for your physical and mental health than an intense weekly workout you dread.
