## Who Cara Dumaplin Is

Cara Dumaplin is a registered nurse and NICU nurse with over a decade of hospital experience, who built Taking Cara Babies into one of the most widely recognised newborn sleep education resources available. The newborn class is her core product, and it's the one that launched the business — largely through word of mouth from parents who found it genuinely useful.

The course is not a sleep training programme. Cara is explicit about this: the newborn phase (0–4 months) is not appropriate for behavioural sleep training methods. The class teaches you how to work *with* your newborn's biology to maximise sleep, not how to train a newborn to sleep independently before they're developmentally capable of it.

## What the Class Covers

The course is video-based, roughly 3 hours of content broken into digestible segments. Core topics:

**Understanding newborn sleep biology.** Why newborns sleep the way they do, what determines wake windows, how sleep cycles differ from adult sleep, and why the first 4 months are a unique (and temporary) phase. Understanding the *why* behind your baby's sleep patterns makes the reality feel less arbitrary.

**The EASY routine.** Eat, Activity, Sleep, You (time for yourself). A loose scheduling framework that prevents the common pattern of feeding to sleep and creates some predictability without rigidity.

**Reading tired cues.** What early tiredness looks like vs. overtiredness, and why catching the window before overtiredness matters.

**Soothing strategies.** A structured toolkit of techniques for settling a newborn, ordered by intensity. The goal is using the minimum effective intervention rather than jumping straight to the most stimulating soothing technique.

**Safe sleep practices.** Revisiting safe sleep guidelines in the context of actual parenting challenges — the gap between "always back in their crib alone" and what exhausted parents actually do.

**Helping baby connect sleep cycles.** Why babies wake between cycles and simple strategies to help them string cycles together without requiring full parental intervention.

## The Tone and Approach

What consistently stands out in reviews is Cara's tone: warm, non-judgmental, and realistic. She's not selling a perfect baby or a strict method; she's acknowledging that newborn sleep is hard and offering tools, not promises.

She's also clear about what the class can and cannot do. It sets appropriate expectations: your 6-week-old will still need you at night. The goal is better sleep for everyone, not sleeping through the night.

## Taking It Before Baby Arrives

The class is most useful if you take it in the third trimester rather than in the exhausted newborn fog. The information is easier to absorb, you can discuss it with your partner, and the strategies can be implemented from day one rather than learned under pressure.

## The Follow-On Courses

Taking Cara Babies has follow-on courses for 3–4 months (when sleep typically hits a challenging transition) and older babies (where more formal sleep training methods become appropriate). Many families who find the newborn class useful return for these later.

## Bottom Line

The Taking Cara Babies newborn class is one of the best investments you can make before your baby arrives. Three hours of focused, practical content from someone with genuine expertise, delivered in a tone that doesn't add to parental guilt. Take it in the third trimester with your partner.
