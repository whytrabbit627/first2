## What This Resource Is

Happiest Baby — the company behind the SNOO smart bassinet and the book *The Happiest Baby on the Block* — offers a range of free online content around newborn soothing and sleep. Their Settle & Sleep class and accompanying guides are available on their website without purchase, and without a SNOO.

The core content extends the 5 S's framework from the book into a more structured, visual format — video demonstrations of the techniques, guidance on common soothing challenges, and realistic expectations for the first 12 weeks.

## The 5 S's — A Deeper Dive

If you've read *The Happiest Baby on the Block* or encountered the 5 S's elsewhere, this class adds practical depth. The demonstrations matter: seeing the swaddling technique done correctly (firmly, with arms straight at the sides, not loose) is more useful than reading a description of it. The shushing volume is something most parents underestimate until they see it demonstrated.

The class also addresses:

**Why it works.** The neurological basis for the calming reflex that the 5 S's activate — why specific types of stimulation (white noise, lateral movement, sucking) trigger a quieting response in young infants. Understanding the mechanism makes the technique feel less like a trick and more like a tool.

**When it doesn't work.** The 5 S's work for the majority of crying newborns, but not every baby every time. The class addresses what to try when the initial attempt isn't settling baby, and how to escalate systematically rather than cycling through techniques randomly.

**Swaddling safely.** The SNOO sack design requires a specific swaddle for use in the bassinet, but the class also covers general safe swaddling principles: hips and legs must be free to move (hip-healthy swaddle), the swaddle must not be loose (fall-off risk), and it should be stopped when baby begins to show signs of rolling (typically 2–4 months).

**White noise guidance.** Volume, distance, and type of sound. Most parents use white noise too quietly to be effective. The class gives specific guidance on appropriate volume levels (louder than you'd expect) and why melodic sounds are less effective for sleep than steady, non-melodic white noise.

## Realistic Expectations for the First 12 Weeks

One of the most valuable parts of this content is the framing of what the first 12 weeks look like — and why. The class is explicit that the goal is not sleeping through the night; it's surviving the fourth trimester with tools that make it manageable.

This expectation-setting is genuinely useful. Many parents arrive in the newborn phase with expectations set by social media or by the cleaned-up accounts of friends, and find the reality — multiple nighttime wakes, unpredictable daytime napping, cluster feeding — alarming. Understanding that this is biologically normal rather than a problem to fix makes the experience more navigable.

## Format and Accessibility

The content is available free on the Happiest Baby website. It's video-based, watchable in sections, and doesn't require creating an account for basic access. Total viewing time for the core content is under two hours.

It's worth bookmarking the website before baby arrives and sharing it with your partner — watching together and discussing the techniques in advance is more useful than trying to recall them at 2am.

## No SNOO Required

This is worth emphasising: this content is fully useful and relevant whether or not you own or rent a SNOO. The soothing techniques work with any bassinet, any swaddle, any white noise machine. The class doesn't require the SNOO; the SNOO is simply one application of the same principles.

## Bottom Line

The Happiest Baby Settle & Sleep content is a high-quality, free resource from a team with deep expertise in newborn soothing. Watch it in the third trimester with your partner. The visual demonstrations of the 5 S's are worth more than reading about them. And the realistic expectation-setting about the first 12 weeks is one of the most practically useful things you can consume before your baby arrives.
