## The Problem with Most Diaper Bags

Traditional diaper bags announce themselves immediately — the pattern, the shape, the sheer number of external pockets signalling "baby stuff" from across the street. For partners who want to carry the bag without feeling like they're carrying a baby accessory, or for parents who want something functional at work as well as at the park, most diaper bags don't deliver.

The Skip Hop Forma solves this by looking like a normal backpack. It's a clean, minimal design available in neutral colourways that work in any context. The diaper-bag features are built in rather than bolted on.

## What's Inside

The Forma is designed for organised packing under pressure — because a bag you can't navigate with one hand while holding a baby isn't actually useful. Key features:

**Wipes pocket with magnetic closure.** Accessible from outside the bag, opens with one hand, no fiddling with zippers when you need a wipe immediately. This sounds minor and matters constantly.

**Insulated bottle pockets.** Two external pockets that hold bottles upright and maintain temperature. Wide enough for standard bottles, accessible without opening the main compartment.

**Changing mat.** A fold-out changing mat is built into the bag. It's thin but adequate for most surfaces, and keeps you from scrambling for something to lie baby on in a restaurant or park.

**Laptop sleeve.** A dedicated padded sleeve fits most 13–15" laptops, making this genuinely usable as a work bag. For parents who need to carry work essentials as well as baby gear, this is a meaningful feature.

**Multiple internal pockets.** The main compartment has organised pockets for nappies, wipes, medicine, snacks, and personal items. Everything has a place, which makes repacking faster.

## Backpack vs. Shoulder Bag

The backpack format distributes weight across both shoulders rather than loading one side. For a bag that might weigh 4–6kg when fully loaded, this is a significant ergonomic advantage over a shoulder bag — particularly for parents carrying it for hours. Both parents can wear it comfortably without adjustment.

The bag has stroller straps (clips that attach to stroller handles) for when you don't want to wear it.

## What's Not Perfect

The main compartment zipper runs across the top in a way that requires putting the bag down to fully access it — you can't rummage with one hand the way you can with some top-opening bags. This is a minor workflow issue that most parents adjust to quickly.

The Forma is not waterproof. The material is water-resistant enough to handle light rain but wouldn't protect contents in a downpour.

## When You Need It

The Forma earns its place from the first outing with baby. Have it packed and ready before your due date — one less thing to think about in the newborn chaos. A reasonable standard load: 3–4 nappies, wipes, changing mat (already built in), one change of clothes for baby, muslin cloths, a bottle or formula, and your personal essentials.

## Bottom Line

The Skip Hop Forma is the diaper bag for parents who want functionality without the baby aesthetic. It works equally well for either parent, survives daily use, and doesn't need to be swapped for something else when you go to a work meeting. One of the most practical registry additions available.
